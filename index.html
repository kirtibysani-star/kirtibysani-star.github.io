Edge AI for Emissions & Asset Monitoring

3 production-ready computer vision POCs for a North American energy operator, with a 5-year, $200M value case behind them.

The problem: A large energy operator relied on manual inspections and periodic monitoring to catch emissions and equipment issues, leading to delayed detection, inconsistent reporting, and real regulatory and financial risk.

What I built: Framed the highest-impact use cases from regulatory and operational gaps, then led design-thinking workshops to align stakeholders. Built a 5-year value and ROI model to justify scaling investment.

What happened: Identified $40M+ in historical compliance risk addressable through proactive detection, and modeled ~140K productivity hours and ~$200M in potential loss avoidance over five years.

Edge AI Video Analytics for Mining Operations

Turned raw video feeds into real-time productivity and safety metrics for a mine maintenance shop.

The problem: A mine maintenance shop had no real-time visibility into operational activity, workforce productivity, or safety compliance, and supervisors were working from anecdote, not data.

What I built: Worked with cross-functional teams to identify the operational metrics that mattered most, then supported deployment of computer vision models detecting wrench vs. non-wrench time, dwell time, vehicle classification, wheel chock placement, and post-service cleaning. Designed the analytics dashboard supervisors would actually use, and built the Excel-based value model behind the client-facing ROI case.

What happened: Delivered real-time visibility into vehicle time-in-bay, wrench time per worker, crew size per task, and vehicle throughput, supporting better production monitoring and safety compliance, with a quantified case for scaling to more facilities.

Generative AI · Full-Stack · Vibe Coding

SubcastAI

Turns your unread Substack subscriptions into a personalized daily podcast in under 60 seconds.

The problem: Most people subscribe to 10–20+ Substack newsletters and read fewer than 30% of them. The issue isn't information overload: it's format and filtering. Reading demands focused attention; audio fits into dead time.

What I built: An LLM pipeline (LLaMA 3.1 via Groq) that fetches full article content from Substack RSS feeds, filters for relevance against stated interests, writes a personalized podcast script, and converts it to audio with Microsoft neural text-to-speech, delivered as an MP3 in under 60 seconds. Built on React + FastAPI, deployed on Render.

What happened: A working end-to-end product, from RSS ingestion to playable audio, with zero infrastructure cost by design (users bring their own Groq API key).

RAG · Generative AI · Product Design

LennyAdvisory

A RAG app that assembles a personal board of advisors from Lenny Rachitsky's podcast archive, tailored to your challenge.

The problem: Built for Lenny's Buildathon: given an archive of 50 podcast transcripts and 10 newsletters and one prompt, make something useful out of it. Summaries felt passive; search felt generic.

What I built: An app that takes your specific challenge and company context, then assembles a board of advisors from the archive, each one assigned a role tailored to your situation, with advice grounded in what each expert actually said, closed out with a synthesis. The board-assembly UX (each seat locking in one by one) was a deliberate design choice to make the output feel earned, not just loaded.

What happened: A working product built in a weekend, using Replit and Framer to get the visual weight right: a board of directors shouldn't look like a consumer app.
