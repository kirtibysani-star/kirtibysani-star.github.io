Platform Adoption & Change Management

Diagnosed and drove adoption of an internal digital tools ecosystem across Accenture's 700K+ person global workforce.

The problem: Digital Worker, the internal site housing every digital tool available to employees globally, had low adoption. The site was organized by tool name and text-heavy, so even employees who knew it existed often couldn't find what they needed. I was brought onto the internal Journey and Change Management team specifically to diagnose why and drive it up.

What I built: Led an end-to-end redesign of the Digital Worker portal, restructuring the information architecture around employee intent (what are you trying to do) instead of tool name, and rebuilding content to be concise and interactive. Built and ran an ongoing change-communications cadence, owning channel, content, and timing decisions (company-wide email, Viva Engage, targeted comms, gamified competitions) across 8+ cross-adoption teams every month. Restructured the internal learning hub around the same six existing personas, designing 15/30/60-minute time-to-value tracks and format (sandbox vs. content) per tool.

What happened: All three workstreams rolled up into a 22% increase in sustained platform engagement, measured through site traffic and click-through from the discovery flow, channel-level comms engagement, and completion-to-usage conversion on learning content. This established a repeatable adoption operating model still in use across tool teams.

Owned the full lifecycle, from pre-sales through deployment, for 3 production-ready computer vision use cases at a North American energy operator, backed by a 5-year, $200M value case.

The problem: A large energy operator relied on manual inspections and periodic monitoring to catch emissions and equipment issues, resulting in delayed detection, inconsistent reporting, and real regulatory and financial exposure.

What I built:

Deployment: Ran use case design sessions post-signing to map the client's existing technical architecture and site conditions, translating discovery into a deployable reference architecture. Managed delivery end to end, driving stakeholder alignment, timeline, and risk through to production.

Value case: Identified the value levers most material to the client from their public financial disclosures, validated them through client discovery sessions, then built a 5-year value and ROI model, quantifying impact at the pilot site and at full multi-site scale, including NPV.

What happened: Quantified $40M+ in historical compliance risk addressable through proactive detection, and modeled ~140K productivity hours and ~$200M in potential loss avoidance over five years. The value case became the basis for a follow-on engagement with the client.

Deployment Strategy · Diagnostic Instrument · Full-Stack

AI Deployment Lifecycle Instrument

A four-stage diagnostic tool that scores AI deployment readiness, flags the constraint most likely to stall a rollout, and generates a phased plan and adoption outlook in response.

The problem: Research from Forrester and Anaconda this year found that 88% of AI agent pilots never reach production, driven mainly by unclear ownership, thin data access, and undefined success criteria. Most AI portfolios prove someone can build a model or an agent. This proves the other half: the judgment to diagnose whether an organization is ready to deploy one.

What I built: A four-stage instrument. Discovery screens each use case with an ICE priority score before any deeper work begins. Readiness scores six dimensions, data, process, sponsorship, change appetite, tech maturity, and risk tolerance, and surfaces the single binding constraint most likely to stall the rollout. Rollout Plan generates a phased response built around real exit criteria, tied to whichever dimension is actually weak. Adoption Outlook projects a post-launch trajectory grounded directly in the readiness inputs.

What happened: A fully working diagnostic, built as a single portable file and stress-tested across a range of realistic and edge-case scenarios.

Generative AI · Full-Stack · Vibe Coding

SubcastAI

Turns your unread Substack subscriptions into a personalized daily podcast in under 60 seconds.

The problem: Most people subscribe to 10–20+ Substack newsletters and read fewer than 30% of them. The issue isn't information overload: it's format and filtering. Reading demands focused attention; audio fits into dead time.

What I built: An LLM pipeline (LLaMA 3.1 via Groq) that fetches full article content from Substack RSS feeds, filters for relevance against stated interests, writes a personalized podcast script, and converts it to audio with Microsoft neural text-to-speech, delivered as an MP3 in under 60 seconds. Built on React + FastAPI, deployed on Render.

What happened: A working end-to-end product, from RSS ingestion to playable audio, with zero infrastructure cost by design (users bring their own Groq API key).

RAG · Generative AI · Product Design

LennyAdvisory

A RAG app that assembles a personal board of advisors from Lenny Rachitsky's podcast archive, tailored to your challenge.

The problem: Built for Lenny's Buildathon: given an archive of 50 podcast transcripts and 10 newsletters and one prompt, make something useful out of it. Summaries felt passive; search felt generic.

What I built: An app that takes your specific challenge and company context, then assembles a board of advisors from the archive, each one assigned a role tailored to your situation, with advice grounded in what each expert actually said, closed out with a synthesis. The board-assembly UX (each seat locking in one by one) was a deliberate design choice to make the output feel earned, not just loaded.

What happened: A working product built in a weekend, using Replit and Framer to get the visual weight right: a board of directors shouldn't look like a consumer app.
